Eligibility

If you are a regular employee scheduled to work 30 or more hours per week, you’re eligible for benefits. Twitter covers 100 percent of your payroll contributions for medical, dental and vision coverage for you. You don’t pay one cent.

We’ll also share in paying the cost for the premiums if you want to cover an eligible dependent.

Cover You Plus One

With this option, you can enroll yourself and another adult on Twitter health plans.

You may enroll your:

  • Spouse (same or opposite sex)
  • Domestic partner (same or opposite sex)
You may also cover your dependent children under Twitter’s health plans.

“Plus One” Coverage No Longer Available

Beginning November 1, 2014, our health plans will no longer offer "+1 adult recipient” coverage. This means we can no longer offer medical coverage to non-dependent adults such as parents, step-parents, in-laws, siblings, step-siblings, etc. Of course, you may still add a spouse or domestic partner to the plan, as well as dependent children.

If you already have an adult recipient on the plan, those adult recipients will be "grandfathered" – and the plan will continue to offer them coverage.

Keep an eye out!

If you enroll a domestic partner (same or opposite sex), you will pay taxes on the value of the benefit. This is also known as imputed income.

What's imputed income?

Imputed income is the term the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) applies to the value of any non-cash, employer-provided benefit that should be considered as taxable wages for the purposes of calculating taxes. This amount is added to your paycheck each period and you pay taxes on that amount. Also, your per-pay-period deduction for your domestic partner is taken post-tax.

How to Enroll

  1. The next open enrollment will be later this year in the Fall/Winter of 2015 for benefits effective on January 1, 2016.
  2. Visit Go/Workday and enter your User Name and Password.
  3. Click the Benefits worklet to access the Benefits Election page.
  4. Select your benefits on each page. If you want to save your progress, click on Save for Later at the bottom of the page.
    • Once you’ve selected all your benefits, you will be directed to summary page.
  5. Double check to see if all your benefit elections are accurate. Then, click the “I agree” checkbox and hit the Submit button.
  6. Click the Print button for a print out of your benefit elections.
  7. Visit Go/Workdayhelp if you have any questions about enrolling through Workday. For other benefits-related questions, use the link on Birdhouse to reach the Tweep Support Center, or visit go/tsc.

Important Reminders

Your elections are final

Any elections you make during your enrollment will be final. You won’t be able to change or modify your elections till the following plan year during open enrollment, unless you have a qualifying life event.

Re-enroll in a Flexible Spending Account

You must re-enroll every year to contribute to a Flexible Spending Account.

Imputed income for your domestic partner

If you add a domestic partner who is not a dependent on your tax return, you will pay taxes on the value of the benefit. Your per-pay-period contribution toward your domestic partner will also be considered post-tax.
